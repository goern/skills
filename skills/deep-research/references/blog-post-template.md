# Blog Post Template — Deep Research Summary

Use this template for the companion blog post. It distills the full research
report into a quick 1-2 minute read written in first-person perspective.

---

```markdown
---
title: "<Catchy, specific title — what's the one thing the reader should take away?>"
date: <YYYY-MM-DD>
research_report: "<path to the full research report>"
tags: [research, <2-3 topic-specific tags>]
---

<Opening hook: 1-2 sentences that frame *why* I looked into this and why the reader
should care. Set the scene with a question, a trend, or a surprising fact.>

<!-- truncate -->

## What I Found

<2-3 paragraphs covering the most important findings. Each paragraph should follow
the pattern: context → finding → why it matters. Bold key terms and results.
Cite sources inline as [Source N] — these must match the full report's bibliography.>

## What Surprised Me

<1 paragraph highlighting the most unexpected or counterintuitive finding.
This is the "cocktail party fact" — the thing the reader will remember and share.>

## The Bottom Line

<1-2 sentences: the actionable takeaway or the open question that remains.
End with a pointer to the full report for readers who want depth.>

---

*This is a summary of my full research report: [<report title>](<relative path>).
That report includes [N] verified sources and a detailed analysis.*
```

## Style Guidelines

- **Length**: 250-500 words (1-2 minute read)
- **Perspective**: First person ("I researched...", "I found...", "What surprised me...")
- **Tone**: Conversational but technically accurate — like explaining findings to a smart colleague over coffee
- **Structure**: Hook → Findings → Surprise → Takeaway
- **Citations**: Use the same [Source N] references as the full report, but sparingly (2-4 max)
- **No filler**: Every sentence should either inform or engage. Cut ruthlessly.
- **No methodology**: Don't explain *how* the research was conducted — that's in the full report
